Risotto

Description

Seafood risotto is a creamy Italian rice dish made by slowly cooking arborio rice in broth while stirring continuously. It often includes shrimp, scallops, mussels, or clams for a fresh, ocean-inspired flavor. The rice releases starch as it cooks, creating a naturally creamy texture. It’s rich, comforting, and layered with delicate seafood flavor.

Ingredients
  • Rice
  • Broth: Slow-added, warm, savory liquid (chicken, vegetable, or fish) creates the creamy, velvety sauce.
  • Onions, shallots, or garlic are sautéed in butter or olive oil.
  • White Wine
  • Cheese & Finishing Butter: Parmesan cheese and extra butter are stirred in at the end
Steps
  1. Heat the Stock: In a medium saucepan, bring the stock to a simmer, then reduce heat to the lowest setting to keep it hot, but not boiling.
  2. Sauté Aromatics: In a large, heavy-bottomed skillet or Dutch oven, melt 2 tbsp butter with olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion/shallot and cook until soft and translucent
  3. Toast the Rice (Tostatura): Add the rice to the pan. Stir constantly for 1–3 minutes, until the edges of the grains are translucent but the center is still opaque.
  4. Deglaze with Wine: Pour in the white wine. Stir until it is completely absorbed by the rice.
  5. Add Stock Gradually: Add one ladleful (about ½ cup) of hot stock to the rice. Stir frequently, allowing the rice to absorb the liquid.
